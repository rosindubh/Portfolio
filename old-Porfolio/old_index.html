Computers

I don't know why I became interested in computers they wern't really a thing when I was growing up more science fiction that science fact. I put it down to some magazines left in the pub my Mother ran. I read the articles and was facinated with them so I got one for my 20th birthday. This was the machine before the BBC Micro and was called the Acorm Atom. I got it in kit form and built it in my bedroom. Acorn if you don't know developed the ARM microprocessor, and if you don't know what that is I suggest you Google it, there is almost certainly one in your moble phone.

Hi-Fi

My other big interest is Hi-Fi. Something lost on most people who use streaming services or listen to Mp3s. The compression on these soundfiles means you are missing out on the wonders that the sound engineer spent hours tweaking to give a perfect stereo soundstage. I still listen to vinyl and I enjoy it's more relaxing and open sound. I know it lacks the speed and convineince of the digital era, but until you hear a quality Hi-Fi system you don't know what you are missing. Believe me you will think you have been listening with cotton wool in your ears when you do.

My reasons for Joining CodeNation

I've been interested in computers and coding since I built my first computer in 1980. This was a very basic machine by todays standards having 2K of RAM and a 2Mhz processor. It was programmed using Assembly Language. I have always wanted a career in coding but in the past without a university degree nobody would consider you. Due to the digital skills shortage that the UK now has this seems to be changing. So I am hoping to gain enough skills to market myself and gain employment in the industry. My background is in the digital print sector so my skills are mainly in digital colour print technology, the TCP/IP suite of protocols and Linux. So if you want to know how a Colour or Black and White printer works, I'm your man. I was for almost 20 years the B/W Colour Specialist for Canon Uk where I received most of my training. Be it powder, wax or ink I've done them all.

My Targets

I am hoping to add HTML, CSS and Javascript to my portfolio of skills along with MySql and MongoDB,. I am comfortable with Python, Linux, Bash and have dabbled with C and Rust. However my biggest weakness is in HTML and CSS and I am hoping to greatly improve my skills in the use of these on this course.
